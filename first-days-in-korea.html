First Days in Korea

erikburk 22 February 2026

Man this place is so cool and theres just so many things to explore and everything and its so cool and i love it and yeah

Theres so much to write about and stuff but basically everything is off to a really good start I think. Most of the courses seem really good and the university campus is just kinda crazy. For now it's still definitely in the honeymoon phase, so I don't know how I will feel in a month, but I love it here. There is a school cafeteria and shared rooms in a dorm and everything. Man it's crazy how Korea feels more American than Canada.

Yesterday I went to a traditional Korean spa, called a jimjilbang (? spelling). It basically one big naked party where you sit in a sauna with a bunch of completely naked Korean men being everything from 6 to 80 years old. Best thing was just talking to the old Korean men and laughing while basically none of us understand what the other is saying. Shoutout breaking language barriers by being butt ass naked. Today I had my first courses. Honestly they seem waaay better than in Montreal, though there are basically only Koreans in my classes. Me and one german guy is going strong in two courses though.
