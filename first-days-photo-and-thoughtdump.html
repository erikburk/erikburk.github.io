First Days Photo & Thoughtdump & A sign of life

erikburk 4 September 2025

uh so i had this all written up but forgot to ever post it lol

Hey blog hope yall are doing great !!!

It has now been five full days in Montreal. Today was my first class and the everyday life is slowly coming together. I have moved in to the apartment and have found courses that won't interfere with each other. And uh yeah n stuff

For the first days i had to stay at the hostel, since the room wasn't free yet. I didn't know it beforehand but i stayed in the middle of the gay village. Very cool vibe and it felt kinda crazy that a city was so on board with being pro lgbtq. There were signs everywhere saying "we accept everyone" etc.

The first full day, I went out into town with a guy from the hostel who was just visiting. We took a tour of old Montreal (very not old) and just meandered around the downtown area. It felt a bit crazy that I was actually in Canada, and even more crazy that everything was in french but still looked almost like New York. For lunch we of course had a poutine. The conclusion was that it was alright for the first half but was kinda tiring for the second. I think it would be better as a side dish (i.e. basically being dirty fries). I atleast took the smoked meat version, which I think helped a bit, but everything still felt rather same-y. A bit disappointing unfortunately. I have photos, but they are on the camera and I can't bother to transfer them right now lol

In the evening i went to the first supermarket on my trip and bought some dinner. Quite alright, costing me about 7$ for three or so meals.

In the evening the previous tenant told me that the room was empty and free for me to use. I didn't have the energy to move everything immmediately, but atleast I could do one round of luggage. Bad idea. Since I didn't have a sim card, I couldn't find the best route with public transport, which meant that I had to walk with the mot unstable suitcase ever for a good 30min or so before actually arriving at the apartment. It was horrible. I just hated the huge streets filled to the brim with cars, all the noise and just kinda missing home. I went to the apartment and basically just turned in the door after leaving the bag, to go back to the hostel.

The second day we went to the museum of fine arts in town. Wasn't anything groundbreaking, and im not the biggest art guy. But there were some international students going there and me, desperate to find anyone to hang out with, of course said yes to go. But yeah it was alright. I was more impressed by the cityscape around the museum. It really felt right out of a movie set in a big american city.

In the evening there was a big rush because i learnt at 15 that Labor day apparently is like super closed and the whole city just kinda shuts down. So i had two hours to get everything i needed for the next two days. Later on i bought a bike of a second hand site💀 a couple days later, I can confirm that this was a great cop and i love the bike. I didn't know it at the time of purchase but it's apparently an old brand made in Canada and everything. Very happy with purchase

The bike was taken to the apartment immediately, and I again had to go back to the hostel by metro. This time, however, I didn't have any stupid bags and the sky was a beautiful pink. When packing down the things in my room in Sweden, I took some magazines from the communal advertisement trashcan postbox in the building. One of them was a snerikes magazine with an article about two students being on exchange. One of them was in Paris, and talked about how all the annoying stuff just kinda disappear - there is no Paris syndrome as an erasmus student, and all the rats are just "a rustic detail". I was starting to agree with her when walking back and listening to Frank Ocean among the cute brick buildings and spiral staircases. 

Anways, so there has been way more time than five days when I am writing this paragraph, and a lot more has happened. I have just kinda forgot to update the blog about things. But will def add more posts in the coming week !!!! Anyways yall have a great day and now it's time for the ad read.
