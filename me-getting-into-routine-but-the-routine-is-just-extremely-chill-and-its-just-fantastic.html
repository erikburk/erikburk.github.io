Me getting into routine but the routine is just extremely chill and it's just fantastic

erikburk 17 March 2026

Whats up yall this is probably the longest streak I have ever consistently updated the blog ever! Huge celebration inbound for this one

Anyways, last week has been pretty good. Classes are still somewhat boring and just full of repetition, but I hope that starting from next week (maybe), there will be new things in all the classes. In the ones with new things, everything is also surprisingly easy. Like, I have barely studied outside of class easy. I talked to a guy who did an exchange in Taiwan about it and he had the same feelings - he said that that's kinda the norm in Asia. I don't know how true that is but definitely an interesting that it's like this in both Korea and Taiwan. This definitely goes against all my prejudices regarding east asians and studying. Anyways, the content is mostly very interesting. It's basically like I'm listening to podcasts about semiconductor technology instead of actually doing any work. Fantastic.

I also love that Fridays are free. Since there is basically (for now) no studying at all, I just get a three-day weekend. Which is so incredibly nice if you actually want to travel somewhere. So much less stress for weekend trips, and like - it's just possible to go  places. Since Korea is such a small country, that makes it even better.

Speaking of  weekend trips, this time we went to Seoul. It was great. We rented an apartment close to Hongdae. The area seems kinda like Södermalm in vibes. Lots of male manipulators and hipster places around. Coming from Montreal, I felt right at home. I mostly hung out in a big flea market area, looking for a Walkman MP3 player. While I had a great time looking through multiple-story shops filled from floor to ceiling of digital cameras and Nintendo DS lites and going through piles of 2000KRW (=13SEK) clothes, I didn't find a single MP3 player. I am close to giving up and just looking for it when (stay tuned!) I go to Japan, or ordering on Ebay. Because it seemed like the prices were pretty much as you can find online. Other than that, we went out clubbing, had Korean BBQ and went to a night market. And man, that night market took a lot of work to find. We had found one place on the internet, which seemed good. When we got there, there was absolutely nothing there. We asked so many strangers, including a policeman giving us a lecture on not smoking in a public area. He actually gave us some directions on where to go, but we never found anything. After maybe 30 mins of just wandering around aimlessly, we kinda gave up and took the bus back to Myeongdong, no. 1 tourist spot in Seoul. We had a great time there, being hungry from all the wandering and searching. Hotteok is a fantastic snack.

Yesterday, I also took my bike for it's first real ride. I biked for about 10km along the river, and came across this super beautiful traditional village. It's Unesco protected and everything. The plan is to bike all the way to Gyeongju and back on Friday. That should be around 30km one way. We'll see if I succed and if theres an okay bike lane all the way there or not. Stay tuned for the issue!

Anyways I hope you have a wonderful week back in Sweden/Europe in large/North America. Love yall puss puss <3
