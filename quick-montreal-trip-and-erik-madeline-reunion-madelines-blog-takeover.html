Quick Montreal Trip and Erik + Madeline Reunion! (Madeline’s Blog Takeover)

erikburk 19 October 2025

Hello, cutiepie blog!

It’s Madeline, and I have just returned from a wonderful trip to see Erik in Montreal. You know what that means… I get to take over the blog for this week! Yippie!

Day 1

I couldn’t sleep very much on the plane, and, since I had left at midnight from California, I was running on only one hour of sleep once I arrived in Montreal. There was a long walk from the metro station to Erik’s apartment, but I couldn’t complain, even if I was half asleep. It was so lovely to walk through the endless tree-lined streets, filled with brick buildings and students chatting and rushing to who-knows-where. The autumn colors were so vibrant, and the piercing cold was a nice change from the heat in California.

After a long nap at Erik’s place, I went to his school to meet him. The bus ride was just as lovely as my earlier walk, and I sat outside of his school once I arrived, admiring the sunny autumn day. Eventually, he called me after he had finished his test, and I made a very sleepy attempt to go up the insane amount of stairs in his school. After what seemed like an endless journey, I turned a corner and, suddenly, there he was! We ran to meet each other (scaring some random guy in the process) and stood and hugged each other for so long. It was so perfect, with the cold wind and the warm sun and the leaves and us together again.

We went on a long walk up to Mont Royal, passing by such a beautiful graveyard and a lake with many cute squirrels and seagulls. We had such a fun time adventuring. At the top of the lookout, we stood for such a long time just admiring the view. It was so beautiful that I never wanted to leave. The city looked so peaceful.

Eventually, though, we went back down again, walking the entire way back. We stopped by a cute book shop and then went home. I met all his roommates for the first time, and they were all so kind and welcoming! I was very grateful that they all made me feel so comfortable. We made galettes and brownies together, and I ate and conversated while mostly asleep. Eventually, we went to sleep, and it was the end of a wonderful first day.

Day 2

The next morning, we had a little galette breakfast and then went grocery shopping for some bagels. We came back and had a really yummy bagel lunch together and forced his roommates to try some Sour Patch Kids sugar cookies we got for $1. Afterwards, he went to class, and I went to the Montreal Museum of Fine Arts where I spent hours looking at all the beautiful oil paintings (and avoiding the modern art).

Erik met me downtown when he was finished with his class, and we did a bit of shopping. We went to Krispy Kreme and Erik tried it for the first time. I also forced him to wear the cute little hat. After that, we went to the Gay Village, got some things at the dollar store, wandered around, and then went to Old Montreal. We saw the whole skyline just after the sun had set, and the entire sky was pink behind the buildings. We meandered down to the main old street where we listened to music, got some matching post cards, ate some free apples that we got in a shop, and talked for such a long time. It was such a cute and lively area!

Afterwards, we took the metro to a different area closer to Erik’s place where we got some smoked meat sandwiches at the deli shop that Celine Dion owns. It was pretty good, but our consensus was that it wasn’t worth the money and was nothing too special. But it was fun to try once! On the way home, we got some pistachio and raspberry ice cream and ate outside before going home and going to sleep.'

Day 3

We woke up and had breakfast before going out to grab a donut and heading to Little Italy. We spent a few hours at the market, looking at all the shops and debating on whether I should buy maple syrup or not. Erik bought a cute little monstera plant, which is yet to be named (sorry, Sandro, but it must be named).

The buses were at strange spots, so we decided to just walk back instead of trying to search for them. But, luckily for us, the weather was great, and we came across so many interesting shops. There was one we found with two cute dogs just running around which I spent so much time petting, and some others that had cats and were definitely owned by throuple lesbians (iconic).

We had a bagel lunch again once we got back and then went back out again to do some more shopping. We went to so many thrift shops and had a lot of fun going through the beautiful autumn avenues with all the hipster cafes and people wandering about. I bought a butterfly chain belt, and then we walked back again towards Erik’s apartment to grab some groceries. I even found some Peace Tea, a total throwback, so we obviously purchased a couple of cans along with an Arizona Iced Tea.

I did my makeup when we got back and Erik cooked pasta for us, and then we rushed off to the Bladee concert. The crowd was so overwhelming at the venue and there were so many tall and insanely annoying guys pushing me out of the way and going directly in front of us. Luckily for me, I can speak Swedish, so I had a great time talking shit about them right behind their sweaty backs. Later in the concert, Erik and I decided to go on a mission to get to the front, so I happily pushed through and elbowed all of the guys who had pushed me out of the way earlier. They were also so rude and so boring! Everyone was just recording and standing there. No moshing, no nothing. We got pretty close to the front which I was very proud of. Apparently, on the way out, Erik said he heard some guys talking bad about me since I had pushed them out of the way, even though they were the same ones who pushed me earlier. Little did they know, I was talking shit about them way earlier. It’s also kind of a skill issue if you’re getting beaten out and pushed around by a 175cm girl. It’s like a reward that they were so mad at me xoxo. But Bladee was great anyways, despite having a freaky fanbase sometimes. (Unfortunately, however, he did not play Mallwhore Freeestyle or I’m Goofy. A sad, sad day for humanity.)

Day 4

We got up early the next morning and took the bus downtown, where we got some quick Tim Hortons. I was super surprised by the fact that their breakfast sandwich didn’t make me want to vomit (I’m looking at you, McMuffins), and the other little snacks we got were so good! We went to the central bus station and took a long bus ride to Quebec City.

When we got there, we spent forever trying to figure out a walking tour, but eventually went off to see the sights. We saw so many things, like the parliament building, the Plains of Abraham, and so much pretty architecture. We stopped to eat for a bit on the main street where Erik had Wetzel’s Pretzels for the first time, and then we were off again!

We went back on the Plains to look out at the river, and then around where we found a perfect view of the Chateau Frontenac. It was so stunning in the sunlight. We walked down to a little church and to a bustling vendor street, stopping in a little tourist shop to buy some things before exploring the inside of the Chateau Frontenac. It was absolutely gorgeous. We stood by the outlook over the river as the sun set, and then went to go see some street performers who were dressed as lamp people. It was so beautiful and it felt like we were in some vintage children’s storybook, just wandering the streets and seeing mystical performances under pink and orange skies.

We walked through the streets, stopping in little shops along the way. We eventually came across some circus performers who were doing such amazing tricks that we were becoming genuinely concerned about their safety. It was so fun, though.

Afterwards, we searched for a place to eat, eventually settling on poutine from Beaver Tails. It honestly tasted amazing, probably partly because I was so exhausted. We went up to the hostel room after we had eaten and spent hours brainrotting together on Instagram reels and watching random Youtube videos. It was such a fun time, and we had such a wonderful and eventful day together.

Day 5

Erik had to work hard to wake me up the next morning because I was so tired. But, eventually, after many wonderful renditions of “Hotel Breakfast” by Bladee, I woke up, and we went downstairs to have some breakfast. It was very good, and we grabbed some extra things to have for lunch as well.

After we eventually packed up and checked out, we went to the lower part of the city to see a lot of cute shopping streets and then made our way over to the Civilization Museum. There was one half of the museum that was entirely based on indigenous populations in Canada which was so interesting and emotional to see. We took so long getting through that part of the museum and we both learned so much! We had a quick lunch break, and then quickly went through the second half of the museum.

After, we went back up the streets again to the main center to wander around and see the Chateau Frontenac and its lookout again. Once we felt we had looked long enough, we made our way back towards the main bus stop and stopped by a very cute and cozy poke bowl shop. We both got Korean poke bowls and ate entirely in silence. It was so delicious. We went by a store to grab some snacks for the road and then went to the bus stop where we ate and talked for an hour. Then we took the long bus ride back to Montreal and walked back to Erik’s apartment after it had just finished raining.

Day 6

After a cozy breakfast and a slow morning, Erik and I took the bus up to Mont Royal to see the lookout again. It was so beautiful, just as it was before. Then, we went back down the other side of the mountain through the gorgeous orange forest. We stopped by McGill to look at the campus, and it was so pretty! Just as we were about to leave, we saw this girl who was carrying a Normal bag with a Smash chocolate bar inside. We ran up to her to ask about it. She said she was from Norway, gave us the chocolate bar instantly (despite us saying a thousand times that it’s okay), and stood there talking with us for an hour. It was a lot of fun!

After we left, Erik and I went to the Barbie Expo, which I was so excited to see! All of the dolls were so gorgeous, and I wanted to take all of them home. Then, we wandered about trying to find some food, eventually settling on a pizza and blackberry yuzu lemonade from Tim Hortons.

We took the bus to the airport and ate and talked. When we got there, we got a little chicken bowl to eat together. Then we walked over to the U.S. departures area.

erik intermission: they played the subway by chappell roan on the airport speaker systme💀 who is the sad lesbian who though that was a fitting song

We sat and talked for a while, and it felt so sad to say goodbye all over again. But it also felt okay knowing that we had done long distance before, and that it would be alright in the end! I’m so grateful that I took this trip to visit him. It was so lovely and perfect, and the most successful trip I think I’ve ever taken! It was so wonderful to be with Erik again, even if just for a short while. And, after all, it’s only nine and a half weeks until we see each other again. So, we said goodbye to each other, but it’s okay! I’m so happy we got to have a few days together. I had the best time.

Thank you, guys, for reading about our little adventure! I hope it was fun to read through. Love you all xoxo.
