rainydaycore yet its 25c and sunny outside so of course its another photodump

erikburk 22 September 2025

Hey team today has been a nice inside day where I've basically only cooked. I've made a huge chicken soup on a whole chicken and some apple pie for the appartment. It's still very warm most days, but I'm starting to feel the autumn vibes. Others in the appartment have bought two super small cute pumpkins that live in our kitchen, and some leaves are starting to turn yellow and red. So what better way to soak in the autumness than to spend the beautiful day inside and pretend that its cold? I should've probably been studying but guided waves in photonics is so incredibly boring that i really cannot bother. Instead I'm doing the rational thing and writing a silly blog post for the silly little blog. Silly indeed.

Also making soup on an entire chicken feels very barbaric - like it's litteraly one life that I'm eating over ten or so meals. I feel like if i told anyone named sandro they would tell me it's a sign of something. Fortunately I live an ocean away from all the sandros that I know of.

Anyways here are some noteworthy things that have happened the last few weeks:

Bike demonstration for Palestine

I of course did not attend and israel has the right to defend itself and all that jazz. Btw hello US customs and border patrol. It seems very fun to protest while riding a bicycle though.

also just side note im convinved not even quebecois understand each other. Because how could someone ever understand tokébakicitte. Also second side note im scrolling Lexique Quebecois and its amazing

Mont Tremblant

Even though the US of course has the best national parks in North America, Canada comes very close. Me and two others in the apartment rented a car and drove about 2h north to arrive at the national park of Mont Tremblant. It was incredible, both the camping and trekking there, and also the ride there. I still haven't really grasped that I am actually in Canada right now, but that trip definitely helped. 

The nature isn't too dissimilar from Sweden, except that it was way more mountaineous. Maybe it's just my measly Stockholm roots talking though. We did two treks, first about 13km, followed by camping with hot dogs, and second about 5km on day 2. 

 

Biking around the island

This was on my bucketlist to do in Canada - do a longer bike ride. Now I can proudly say that it's done and I will most likely never do it again. The roads were quite bad, cracked and full of potholes. Bike lanes were spotty at best. On occation there were good bike lanes, but then they just kinda stopped and you had to do you best to not crash into the incoming traffic. It was basically no countryside, only either more of the city or some very cliché american suburbs. There were some very cute parts though, but I don't really have any urge to go back or anything. Also, even though cars in the city are very nice towards bicyclists, the same does not hold true for the boonies. I got honked at, people overtook me with 0.5m or less to spare and i was just generally not having a good time sharing the road with the cars. One cool thing however was the return, seeing the skyscraper skyline in the distance and slowly riding through the city. It felt like I was one of those early 20s people moving into the big city trying to find themselves. And uh. Yeah anyways

I had some dreams to bike all the way to Quebec city, Ottawa or even New York, but that seems incredibly far away at this point. I will never im lying again complain about Swedish bike infrastructure after this experience.
