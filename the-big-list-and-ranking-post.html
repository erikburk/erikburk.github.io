The big list and ranking post

erikburk 25 October 2025

Hello blog! Hope yall are doing great<3

I have had a couple of very hectic weeks behind me. First, a full week of only studying and a midterm before the break, then Madline came, and then a break week, consisting of studying for 10h/day more or less to catch up. Meanwhile, my neighbours have been going on week long camping trips and visiting Boston and everything. Mechanical  and computer engineers amirite 

This week has been a bit more chill, but I also had two midterms on the same day, and a couple of projects that needed to be handed in. Polytechnique better be happy that i enjoy physics from this amount of work >:( Anyways, now all the midterm exams and reports are done, and I can finally rest for the weekend! So, in no particular order, here are events and things that have happened since the last post:

  • I saw two concerts: Oklou and Yung Lean. Very cool. About the same audience for both, but way more well-behaved at the Oklou concert. See below for more deets!!!!
  • I did two midterm exams, of which one was at 03:00. I just love kvantmekanik 10hp avancerad nivå so much <3
  • I have started watching the K-drama Hometown Cha Cha Cha with Madeline. It is incredibly cheesy and we mostly just laugh over how stupid it is. But also very fun. Recommend!
  • Also yeah im going back to Sweden after new year's and going to Pohang in South Korea to study next semester. Don't have that much more info as of now but yeah it is happening!
  • I went around street photographing today in a park and there were SO MANY SQUIRRELS like they were litteraly everywhere and just chilling with humans. On one park bench, a woman was litteraly invaded by squirrels climbing all over her. They are also very photogenic, as can be studied in Figure [1].

Concerts

  • Viagra Boys -- Crazy good show. Everyone was just moshing and having a grand old time. I got to sing in their microphone and it was overall just a surreal experience. Also plus points for me not being ready at all to go and just buying the tickets kind of on a whim the same night. The setlist was incredibly good, with a mix between basically all the albums, and it was great chilling with everyones' uncles and aunts. The opening band BOODEGA was also really good, though when they told us they were from New York, everyone just kinda looked at each other a bit awkwardly. Quebecois people are so funny. Verdict: 10/10.
  • Oklou -- Speaking of buying tickets on a whim, I bought tickets to this concert litteraly like 10mins before it started. I had totally forgotten and was ready to study for like and hour and go to sleep early. But instead, I got the calendar notification that it was her show, and hopped onto ticketmaster to find a resale ticket. Luckily, there were tickets for not that much, and I zoomed 7km to the other side of town to make it just in time to catch the last part of the dj opener, which for once was actually really good. She was also amazing. Emotional, lots of silly in-between talk, great performing and very cool stageplay. Amazing crowd, seemingly consisting of drainers and lesbian montrealers. When she asked which language she should speak on stage, everyone shouted FRANCAIS. Loved it. 9/10 just because I couldn't see more of the opener.
  • Yung Lean and Bladee -- yeah they only get one combined entry actually. It was alright, but not much more than that unfortunately. It was just so much better when I saw them in Globen, or at Way Out West. Bigger, more ambitious performance and better crowd. Here, people didn't even know the lyrics. On the Yung Lean show, there was also a couple geeking on some kind of substance, just kinda ruining the vibe for everyone around them. They were pushy, tried to start fights, flicked me in the back of the head because I had told them they were annoying. When the show started though, a guy got so tired of them he pushed them all the way to the back. Then everybody, even Yung Lean, clapped. In total: 5/10, I don't regret going to any of the concerts, but I would also not be sad if I missed them. Fun to have seen them in Montreal though.

Courses

  • Guided Waves in Photonics -- Learning about waveguides and optical fibers (eg. internet cables and stuff). So far, quite a bad course. Bad professor and not that interesting. He makes everything way too complicated compared to other courses that I find on the same subject online. There are also mini-projects in matlab that we have to do, where he gives us functions that we have to use, but they are hidden. So, we cannot see how they work and if they even work at all. It took probably 15 hours to finish the first project, and it was supposed to take 5. Classes are also really bad, he has powerpoints filled to the brim with text and equations, but he doesn't talk about the contents there at all. Instead he blabs on about his research in the field and scribbles new equations on the slides with his writing pad. Probably among the worst courses I've done. 3/10, just because waveguides are cool and the second project was a lot shorter than the first.
  • Quantum Optics -- very hard to gauge if the course is good or not. It is for sure quantum and optics. I have gotten a lot of practice in quantum mechanics, which is good, but the first professor has been quite flummig and not super coherent. He oscillates between different subjects like the simple harmonic oscillator on a mix of different energy eigenstates. Homework is also incredibly hard, and takes way more time than it should. Atleast it is good training. Second half seems cooler though, and actually a lot less quantum. The professor has also changed, and hopefully he is more coherent. In total: 7/10, because it is done all on the board (so far) and I actually like quantum mechanics.
  • Lasers -- Interesting subject, but the teacher is super incoherent. But so far, among the cooler courses I am doing, just based on what I am learning. I just want the professor to get a good nights sleep before the classes so that he can be a bit more awake and ready to teach. 6/10.
  • Microfabrication -- My expectations for this course was sooo high, but so far it's kind of a disappointment. We are learning about different way and technologies to create things on a micro-and nanometer scale. Which is very cool in itself, but it has been more of just an overview of different techniques. This has been my dream to work in since i was like 14, and I think I have come to the realisation that most parts of this just isn't for me. I definitely think lithography, where you use light to create superduper small things, is really cool but I haven't really gotten a good deep dive in the subject in the way I wanted. Maybe the right way is through laser physics and quantum optics. Also, thin film science (creating super thin layers of metals and stuff), and etching (where you remove the right parts of the super thin layer you just created) honestly seems super boring. It is also all done on powerpoint, which is a big negative. However, along the course we are also doing our own microthingymajig step-by-step in labs. So we can be in the school's cleanroom and do our own process, which is really cool. And the professor is really cool. In total: 6/10, but I might have expected way too much out of this.

me when the feature size of my sample is less than 3 micrometers

  •  Life Cycle Analysis -- this course I took kind of just on a whim, because I had a schedule conflict between two other courses. In large, it's a good course, good professors, lots of practice sessions (compared to zero for the other courses), and alongside we are doing our own life cycle assessment -- meaning, we are investigating and comparing the total climate impact between two things. In our case, a cargo e-bike vs. electric vans for delivery. My project group is also really good and everything. Just kinda sad that this kind of engineering is not my cup of tea. In total: 8/10. It's a really good course, but it's kinda in the wrong field for me, and lectures are still on powerpoint.
  • Bonus entry !!!! Kvantmekanik 10 hp Avancerad nivå 1FA352 -- yeah im taking a course at Uppsala at the same time. I already did kind of all the reading for this course during the summer to be ready for quantum optics, but never did it super in-depth. My plan is to read up on the last part of the course when I'm done with everything here, and when I come back to Sweden, and hopefully I can pass the exam. That would help alot with next autumn, but we'll see how it goes. I also did the midterm in that course yesterday. It was online at 9am swedish time, meaning I had to wake up at 3 and write a quantum mechanics exam. I think I did great, but I was also barely awake for the entire 3 hours that I wrote it. Atleast I think I'll pass it. Based on the canvas page, it seems to be a good course and I'm honestly kinda bummed out I'll miss it if I end up passing the exam in January. Overall 10/10.

Food and Restaurants

  • Schwartz -- Smoked meat deli shop I visisted with Madeline. Quite disappointing and quite expensive. It is apparently owned by Celine Dion, who helped them out of a bankrupcy. We don't see the vision. also 15CAD was quite alot for what we got. 3/10
  • Bagels
    • Fairmount Bagel -- probably the most hipster bagel place in town. They have queues to a bagel place, and is open 24/7. Also very old and the place to find Montreal style bagels. Are they any good? Yeah, kind of. For a bakery, it's not outrageously expensive (about 1.40CAD per bagel) and they have lots of cool variants. However, I have come to realize that I don't really like Montreal style bagels. They're just too sweet. 5/10
    • S:t Viateur Bagel -- The second most hipster bagel place in town, and is fighting tooth and nail against Fairmount. Same kinda ordeal. Also open 24/7, has queues, same price and everything. Again, I'm probably just not the target audience for Montreal style bagels. 5/10
    • No name brand -- Very cheap, which is kind of expected. The brand is the ICA Basic of Canada. However, their bagels are quite nice, and as a non-connosieur they are really good. I have tried both the all dressed and full grain bagels, and both are really good. 8/10
    • The one brand that me and madeline bought that was new york style but italian and all-dressed -- Also very good. More expensive than the No-name brand, but also definitively better. Probably the best bagel I have had so far in Montreal (meaning, also, in my life). 9/10
  • McDonald's -- honestly awful. I ate here morning I had to go back to Sweden, and it was on the verge of vomit inducing. Also the vibe was just not it. I miss max </3 1/10
  • Chez Claudette -- Apparently the best poutine place in town. I honestly don't see the vision here. The fries were soggy and they just kinda forgot salt. Though, the portions are really large. 2/10
  • Beaver Tail -- chain restaurant that sells beaver tails (fried dough with sweet toppings) and poutine. The beaver tails are very similar to churros, they're alright I guess. But the poutine !!! Actually the best I've had in Canada. Although, it's just fries, cheese and gravy, so nothing breathtaking, but it is the first poutine I've had in Canada that I actually enjoyed. 7/10
  • Polytechnique's Canteens -- honestly really good for the price. I'm paying like 10CAD to get a poké bowl, or burger/BLT with fries, or a ramen bowl. It's nothing breathtaking, but for the price it's really good. The actual canteen is in an american high school style, which maybe isn't the most fun environment, but that's a small parenthesis. 8/10
  • Iconoglace -- Hipster ice cream sundae spot close to me. Huge creations and they are really good. But almost too huge, if you're not sharing. Also quite expensive -- 11cad for an ice cream. Though, considering the amount I guess it does make sense. 8/10.
  • Tim Hortons -- honestly, both a letdown and a letup. I thought it was more of a breakfast place, with pancakes and waffles and everything. Turns out they just kinda sell everything and nothing at the same time. It's more of a McDonald'sified café. Me and Madeline has some munchkins, a bagel, muffin and a pizza from here. Everything was alright and actually not vomit-inducing, but nothing crazy. The munchkins were dry. Atleast it was quite cheap. 4/10
