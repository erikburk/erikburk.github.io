The (pretty) big biking post

erikburk 31 March 2026

Hey so I actually wrote all this up two weeks ago but never came around to finish it. I am currently in my midterm period, so time is in fact not the most plentiful. Even though everyone says that you will litteraly pass by just showing up, I still feel stressed about it. By Wednesday, after my three tests, everything will be over atleast.

Anyways, enjoy my two-week old blogpost in the meantime - by next week, I will update you on all the fun stuff happening!

Please read this about biking i beg you i swear its interesting

As I said in a previous post, my dream here is to do a long bike trip, preferrably to Seoul. To start off a bit easy, I tried going to Gyeongju the other week, about 50 km round trip. That went really good, and I felt really confident for the longer trip, wherever it took me. There were some really good bike routes along the river, and whenever I had to bike on car roads, everyone felt really kind and didn't go too fast. The plan originally was to bike all the way to Seoul starting thursday afternoon last week. I prepared with routes and was looking up motels and everything on the way. Unfortunately, the air quality was like extremely bad and I did not really want to go all the way to Seoul in full smog. What I tried to do instead was to bike towards Busan. I took the scenic route along the ocean, which would have taken about 2 days of biking in total. Unfortunately, about 40 km in, I got a forest fire warning in my phone and there were forest fire cars driving past me. I tried to wave one down but instead of stopping the woman just smiled and waved and drove past. Apparently it was fine, but I didn't want to take any risks. I biked back to Pohang instead. It was a good first try of the road though, and I really want to try again soon. Stay tuned for that, and for the Seoul bike trip.

Other things that happened during the two weeks

  • I went to Daegu. Daegu is the 3rd or 4th largest city in the country, and everyone here kinda jokes about Daegu that there's nothing to do there or whatever. I have to admit - I was defintely one of them before going there. However, I am now changed. Daegu is a really cool city, and I loved the vibes there. Both me and my roomie Jens agreed that if we were to live here long term, Daegu would be one of the top spots to do so. They also had a huge tech mall with a bunch of camera stores, PC shops and almost everything you could imagine. Since I live for those kind of places, I felt like a kid in a (very expensive) candy store. It was great. Something they didn't have though was MP3 players and Sony Walkmans. Fortunately, Daegu blessed us again with an old man in a random market shop. I found a lil beauty for waaaaay below ebay price. Shoutout mr. old Daegu man. I hope he's doing great.
  • We went hiking close to Pohang. There's a trail called the 12 waterfalls, where you just hike along a mountain pass and see a bunch of waterfalls. It was really cool. The trees still haven't gotten that many leaves, so it definitely looked a bit naked on the trail. Bummer but I guess we just have to go back another time to see it again.
  • We went to Gyeongju world, like an amusement park half an hour away from Pohang. It was a blast. Somehow the queues were way shorter than what I'm used too. The rides were also really good, and snack prices were reasonable. Overall a very good experience, and the price with taxis included was not even entry+free rides back home.

Jens, Ludo and Karro, tell em

I saw this once in Gyckeln and I thought it was a really funny concept, so here is my blatant ripoff of the concent. We all need some guidance in the current trend landscape. To help us in this task, I interviewed two people:

  • Jens-David Olabode, maybe Denmarks biggest trendsetter and zeitgeist icon. He has a solid 21 years in the style game, and never misses a beat.
  • Ludvig Olof Lindefeldt, most peaceful and chill known to man. Rumors say he was sent to Islamabad for peace negotiations. By following this man's teachings, even you might reach your inner trend zen.
  • Karolina Hedberg, knows what's up all the way between Skellefteå and Göteborg. Among the widest reaches in all of Sweden, both geographically and figuratively.

The premise is simple. A thing or concept can be:

  • Hot: You want this. The thing is solidly in and will be so for the foreseeable future.
  • Cooling down: The thing is currently hot, but will not stay so for much longer.
  • Not: The thing is not hot. You would not want to touch it with a 10 meter stick.
  • Heating up: somewhere in between hot and not, and definitely on the uptick. You are ahead of the curve, so to say.
