The weekend of week 44 is almost over!

erikburk 2 November 2025

Hello blog! Another week, another blogpost!!

This week has for sure had its ups and downs. All my midterms are done, and I have basically no school work (for now) except for catching up on everything I've missed from before. However, this doesn't stop me from having lots to stress and worry about -- in November I will have fifteen (15) assignments. Crazy to think that all these professors cannot coordinate and spread out the work for the courses that they know exchange students pick together, even though being litteral colleagues and and sharing the same offices.

I'm also definitely feeling the fact that it's more and more autumn-y now. I've had the months of the nice mild early Montreal autumn now, with pretty warm weather and a long period of trees turning yellow/red and everything generally feeling quite vibey. But as with every late autumn comes the late autumn depression. The leaves are starting to turn brown and are wiltering away. It's getting colder, both outside and in the apartment (as the landlord still hasn't turned on the heating). It's more rainy and darker outside. All the fun things I've planned so far is kinda done and over, and what's left until christmas is just kinda to do school work. November kinda always feels like this, just dark and boring and something you have to wait out until december comes around and makes people automatically more happy. It doesn't get better by the fact that I have crazy much schoolwork and basically won't really have any free time. I really want to be one of those that enjoy the autumn, which I do to an extent. But, when november rolls around it's always just dark and bleak and cold and wet and boring. It feels a lot like the Bob Hund song helgen v.48. The time is just kinda passing by and I'm just waiting for the weekend of week 48 to be over so that December can roll around and make everyone happy again.

Maybe I should've gone to the southern hemisphere for my exchange instead, and just chill out during argentinian spring or something. 

I'm also very much missing Sweden and my life back home at this point. I miss living in a city that okay-ish sized where there's things to do all the time and that feels more spontaneous. I miss my friend network back home. I miss studying at Carolina Rediviva and other old campuses. I being able to go to school or Kalmar and just know that there'll be some friends I can hang out there. Shoutout to nations man they definitely help the student town vibe dramatically. Of course I knew about how the student life was ish in Stockholm before, but I never really realized how much they help the social life in the city. Not only the really cheap bars and clubs, but also the kind of "third space" where you can just hang out with and meet people. Even though Montreal sure is a big city with a lot of things happening, it's not that student focused from what I see, and I definitely don't have the friend network from back home that I could just do dumb stuff with at any point that I wanted. Anyways, when I come back I demand that there shall be a big party. Any hosting abilities in Uppsala is very appreciated. 

There were mainly two things except for  school that happened this week:

La Ronde Amusement Park

Last Sunday me and my friend Lauren went to the local amusement park, for the last day of their halloween special opening thingymajig. It was alright, but honestly a bit too little scary people walking around. Apparently they were way more intimidating earlier years, with (chainless) chainsaws and everything, but it felt quite tame this time around. Maybe it was just the last day and they didn't really have anything left, or cost cutting measures, or the evil politically correct mafia has struck again and stolen their chainsaws. Hard to tell which. Anyways, the rides were pretty cool. It was an amusement park alright. I do think Liseberg in Göteborg is alot better though -- vibe is better and  cooler rides. I miss Balder and Helix </3

At the midpoint we also had a Beaver Tail, which is a (traditionally Canadian ? don't know) pastry consisting of fried dough and some kind of topping. Think like a churros kinda thing, but it's flat and looks like a beaver tail. I had apple pie filling topping on mine. It was actually a lot better than I expected, even though I don't like churros that much. I'd give it a solid 7/10.

Parc Omega, and short roadtrip

Last Tuesday, I went with two other from the apartment and some friends of friends to a huge wilderness zoo kinda thing, called Parc Omega. The day started off really early, when we had to get to the airport to pick up the rental car. By the time we get there it was a huge hassle just to get to the car rental companies including walking across the entire airport and taking the shuttle and everything. About an hour after arrival to the airport, we finally find the rental place, just to find out that the booking we made was invalid and cancelled. Luckily there was another car that we could rent that fit everyone, and we were off.

After about two hours of driving, including lots of Veronica Maggio by demand of Swedish music, we cross the border very quickly into Ontario and stop at a border town to buy groceries. First thing we notice is the Canadian politeness. It was honestly quite crazy how big of a difference there was. The cashier lady just went on and on with small talk, and was so interested in us going to Parc Omega. I hope she has a great day. After buying things for the baguettes that we had bought earlier at the local french bakery in Montreal (because obviously Canadian baguettes would not suffice for French ppl) and some chips, we went on the last stretch, back into Quebec, arriving at the park just a short bit after.

The actual consisted of a trail that you drive on, with loads of wild animals along the way. I'm talking deers, moose, boars, bears, wolves and foxes. First thing we see when we get into the park was a huge collection of deers just waiting for the cars that drive in to feed them with carrots. Animal ethics aside, it was a really cool experience. Apparently they do actually take care of the animals in there and let them live semi-naturally, but if your name is Sandro, please look away. Essentially, they are held in captivity but have all the animals neccesary to kind of simulate an ecosystem kinda thingymabob.

Recipe, as promised from last week - Eriks kinda original tofu shawarma

I was really craving shawarma a couple of weeks ago. However, there aren't that many shawarma/kebab places in town. At least no places with the swedish tio minuter en kvart vibe -- they're all kinda too bougie, and I do not trust a bougie shawarma place run by hipster white people. I'm also trying to buy as little meat as possible, and wanted to try new ideas with tofu. So, here's instead a recipe by a hipster white guy doing very untraditional tofu shawarma!

You will need:

  • 400 g extra firm tofu, preferrably genuine Quebecois
  • 2 tbsp ml soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce 
  • 2 tbsp oil (I use canola oil)
    Realistically, this is just your standard tofu marinating ingredients. Change as you like.
  • 1-2 dl yoghurt/sourcream
  • Juice from 1/2 lemon
  • Shawarma spice mix
  • (if preferred) Other spices, such as cayenne pepper
  • Some kind of flour (e.g wheat, maize, potato)

Steps to make it

  1. Grate the tofu with a box grater on the large side into a big bowl.
  2. Add all the other ingredients except the flour, making sure to add a generous amount of shawarma spice mix. Blend and let sit to marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Add a bit of flour and blend again. Spread out thinly on a baking tray covered in a baking sheet. Preferrably, only one layer thick of tofu.
  4. Put in the oven on the broil mode (ie. heat only from the top element) as high as it can go. Cook until the edges are starting to burn.
  5. Done! Serve as you wish, eg. liba bread with vegetables and sauce, in a taco bread with fries if youre fr*nch 🤢, or as a taco sandwich.

Make vegan: Change yoghurt for vegan yoghurt, and oyster sauce for vegan oyster sauce. Make gluten-free: do not use wheat flour.

Other random things on my mind 

"Digital" versus actually owning things

Before I left for Canada, I just had a craving to have a physical cookbook. Of course, I didn't really have to crave, as there was a copy of Bonniers Stora Kokbok from the 1980s ish sitting in my bookshelf, just screaming to be used, while I just use Arla or ICA websites whenever I need recipes. So that was for sure kind of on me.

However, it goes kinda further than that. I really want to try to own more things in my life. Be it, having mp3 files intead of spotify, setting up my own cloud service where I have full control of the actual files instead of Google Drive, Google Photos or Onedrive etc

My biggest dream is to have my own server in the closet of a future apartment, where I can just kinda have everything I want. It's not really about preserving data or whatever, but I just like the thought of actually having the files instead of using a streaming service or something similar. Realistically, what difference does it make? Not that much. But it just feels better. Also, it prevents big tech companies from spying on your photos and training AI models of your listening and watching habits, and creating even better AI slop machines from the photos and videos on your phone. Scawy stuff.

It also ties into the whole attention economy debacle and choosing yourself what to watch or listen to. It feels more human that way.  I've been trying to cut down on social media use as much as possible this year. And while it works out pretty well in Sweden, it's definitely a lot harder when I'm abroad. I've had to redownload instagram just because some student organizations have group chats there. It's quite frustrating really -- you can do as much as you want yourself, but in the end you're kinda tied to whatever the people around you are using if you want to stay in touch. Maybe I should just leave society and return to monkey. I've also wanted to use a dumbphone for a while now, but that's even more out of reach. For now, Whatsapp is kind of a requirement which complicates things alot. In Sweden, Swish and stuff I could realistically live without, by just having physical cash on me, or sending the swish when I get home from a smartphone that I just have swish/bankid. However, here I'm kinda stuck with using Whatsapp for more or less all communication. There are some feature phones that advertise whatsapp compatibility, but as far as I've seen, support is dropped as of recently. When I come back to Sweden in June, I will definitely try harder, but for now a smartphone is very important to have.

Yeah actually i just had one thing on my mind and that was it

Anyways, until next sunday, hope yall have a good november week that is not too depressing! Mwah!
